# Lighting Reference

## Basic Lighting Setups

### Three-Point Lighting
- **Key Light**: Main light source, primary illumination
- **Fill Light**: Soften shadows from key light
- **Back Light**: Separate subject from background

### Advanced Techniques

| Technique | Description | Mood |
|-----------|-------------|------|
| Rembrandt Lighting | 45° key, shadow on opposite cheek forms triangle | Dramatic, classic |
| Split Lighting | Key light on exactly one side of face | Mysterious, intense |
| Loop Lighting | Key at 30-45°, small shadow from nose | Flattering, natural |
| Butterfly Lighting | Key directly in front, shadow under nose | Glamorous, beauty |

## Film Lighting Styles

- **Chiaroscuro**: High contrast, deep shadows (Noir)
- **High Key**: Bright, minimal shadows (Comedy)
- **Low Key**: Dark, dramatic (Thriller, Horror)
- **Naturalistic**: Practical lights, subtle (Drama)
- **Cinematic Neon**: Colored rim lights, cyberpunk aesthetic

## Color Temperature

- **Daylight**: 5600K (blue-ish)
- **Tungsten**: 3200K (orange-ish)
- **Golden Hour**: 2500-3500K (warm sunset tones)
- **Blue Hour**: 8000-12000K (pre-dawn/post-sunset)
